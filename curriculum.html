Lesson Plan 1: Greetings and simple polite words

Objective: Learn basic greetings and polite words used in everyday conversation.

Video Link: Learn Spanish Greetings | English to Spanish | Rock 'N Learn or view in site:

Introduce the words: “Hello", “Good morning”, “Good afternoon”, “Good evening”, “Goodbye”, “How are you?”, “My name is”, “Pleased to meet you”, “Fine, thank you”, “What is your name”, “What’s going on?”, “Nothing”, “Yes”, “No”

Greeting Circle: Students stand in a circle and greet the person next to them using phrases like “Hello", “Good morning”, “Good afternoon”, “Good evening”, “Goodbye”, “How are you?”, “My name is”, “Pleased to meet you”, “Fine, thank you”, “What is your name”, “What’s going on?”, “Nothing”, “Yes”, “No”

Next Steps: Each student repeats the greeting until it goes around the circle. Could do a second round and also use a ball: have child 1 start and say a greeting and question then throw it to child 2 who greets them as well and then asks a question to the next child they decide to throw the ball to and so on (mentioned in website lesson plans)

Introduction (~10mins)

Use flashcards with images or simple scenarios (e.g., someone handing over an item with "Thank you").

Main Activities (~20)
Charades
  1. Each team gets in a line, facing in the same direction.
  2. The child at the end of the line (Child A) will turn around, and a volunteer will show them a picture of a greeting to act out.
  3. When Child A has seen the picture, they will turn back around and tap on the shoulder of the child in front of them (Child B).
  4. Child B will turn around to face Child A, and try to mimic the actions of Child A. Child A cannot talk.
  5. When Child B has understood, they will turn back around and tap on the shoulder of the child in front of them (Child C) and repeat the same action.
  6. The process repeats until the child at the front of the line (Child Z) has seen the action.
  7. Child Z will then make a guess as to what the greeting might be, based on the actions.
  8. Each team will have a different greeting to act out, and the team to guess correctly in the shortest amount of time wins.
  9. When everyone has had a turn, teams will count up their points, and the team with the most points wins.

Lesson Plan 2: Colors

Objective: Introduce basic color vocabulary in English.

Warm-up Activity

Introduction: Start with a brief greeting in English ("Hello, good morning!") followed by a short introduction of the day's topic ("Today, we will learn colors").

Icebreaker: Ask the students to say the name of their favorite color in Spanish.

Intro to Basic Vocab, Topic: COLORS, Words introduced: Red, Blue, Yellow, Green, Orange, Purple, Pink

Click for Color activity (canva)
Classroom objects Flashcards in Colorful Illustrative Creative 3D Style by Eva Winchester

Vid link: Spanish Color song + Learn English and Spanish Colors | Nursery Rhymes by EFlashApps or view in site:

Repetitions: Have the children repeat each color's name multiple times, associating it with the object or card shown. Can quiz them by saying a color in spanish and having them reply the same color in English

Practice through Activities (~15 mins)

Color Hunt: Ask the children to find an object in the room that matches the color you name.

Objective: Reinforce color vocabulary by associating it with real-life objects.

Color Dictionary

Materials: paper & scissors(or white cards), something to draw on(paper/whiteboard), 6-10 colored writing utensils, object to draw from (cup, hat, box, bag, etc).

Preparations: cut out 6-10 cards from paper(or use white cards) and write down the English & Spanish name of a color on the front of each card (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple, pink, brown, black, gray). Put all of the cards into an object to draw from. Then place the paper/whiteboard & colored utensils next to the cards.

How to play

  1. Teachers picks a random student
  2. The chosen student faces away from the class and draws a card. Then they pick the writing utensil to match the color
  3. The student makes a drawing with that color and writes its Spanish name next to it
  4. Then they show the class what they drew and the first child to say the English name of the color gets the card(if no one answers correctly the student reads the answer and puts the card back)
  5. Repeat the steps until all cards have been drawn and given to a student
  6. The student with the most cards wins the game

Can then have students sort the separate colors and hang the drawings up somewhere in the classroom next to the english name for each to help students remember them

Intro to simple sentences (~10 min)

Focus: Form simple sentences like "This is red," "The apple is green."

Pair Work: Have the children point to a colored object in the classroom and say the color in English, practicing with a partner.

Might want to first introduce the verb “is” and explain a bit about pronouns/articles in speech

Review and Recap (~10)

Quiz: Hold up colored flashcards and ask the children to name the colors in English

Reinforcement: Repeat the colors learned, asking students to recall them without visual aids.

End with "Goodbye" in English

Lesson Plan 3: Parts of the Body

Objective: Introduce basic vocabulary for parts of the body in English.

Vid link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Zbh3k7rwdoQ or view in site:

Additional Song: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZanHgPprl-0 or view in site:

Warm Up (~5)

Introduction: Greet the students and introduce the topic ("Today, we will learn the names of some parts of the body").

Icebreaker: Ask the children to point to different body parts as you name them in Spanish.

Intro to basic vocab (~15)

Topic: Parts of the body

Words: Introduce 7 basic body parts (head, arm, leg, hand, foot, eye, ear).

Visual Aids: Use a diagram of the body or point to your own body parts as you say each word.

Repetitions:Have the children repeat each body part's name multiple times, associating it with the part of their own

Practice Through Activities (15 minutes)

Activity 1: 'Simon Says': Play "Simon Says" using the new vocabulary (e.g., "Simon says touch your head").

Objective: Reinforce body part vocabulary in a fun, interactive way.

Materials: paper & scissors(or white cards), writing utensil

Preparations: cut out 7 cards from paper(or use white cards) and write the name of a body part(head, arm, leg, hand, foot, eye, ear) in English with Spanish translation on the front of each card. (the cards are an optional part of the game, if the students know the English name for body parts well enough then the cards aren’t needed)

How to play

  1. The students stand up and spread out a couple of feet away from each other and the teacher stands in front of the class.
  2. The teacher then starts the game by calling out “Simon dice tocar tu…” and adds the English name for a body part. “Simon dice tocar tu head” (they also have the option to use the cards)
  3. The students then have to touch that body part. But the rule is the students only do as the teacher says if they say Simon dice(Simon Says) before saying the command. For example, if the teacher had said “tocar tu head” without Simon dice, then the students who touched their head would be eliminated and out of the game. The students who touched the wrong body parts would also be eliminated. (The teacher can also add commands to freeze and dance in between to make the game more fun.)
  4. The teacher continues to give different commands(speeding up each command will make the game more challenging and fun) until there is one student left.
  5. That student wins the game
  6. After playing the game once or twice the students can take turns in the teacher’s place giving the commands for each game while using the cards.

Activity 2: Drawing: Ask children to draw a simple person and label the body parts in English.

Objective: Practice vocab through drawing and writing

Introduction to Simple Sentences (10 minutes)

Focus: Form simple sentences like "This is my hand," "I have two legs."

Pair Work: Have children practice the sentences with a partner, pointing to their own or each other’s body parts. if they are unfamiliar with using “this is my” or “I have” could do an activity showing different animals that also have all of the body parts mentioned and having them see the multiple meanings for what each body stands for not just on a human.

Review and Recap (10 minutes)

Quiz: Point to a body part and ask the children to name it in English (could have each kid pat their “arm, leg, etc.” and have other try to quickly say out what they are patting to make a bit more fun)

Reinforcement: Repeat the body parts, asking students to recall them without pointing

Lesson Plan 4: Shapes

Objective: Introduce basic shapes in English.

Vid link: Learn the Basic Shapes in Spanish: Circle, Square & Triangle (Círculo, Cuadrado y Triángulo) or view in site:

Warm-up (~5)

Introduction: Greet the students and introduce the topic ("Today, we will learn shapes").

Icebreaker: Ask the children to name shapes they know in Spanish. Can use shapes canva below that excludes the english names for this part

Click to see canva

Introduction to Basic Vocabulary (15 minutes) Topic: Shapes. Words: Introduce 5 basic shapes (circle, square, triangle, rectangle, star)

Visual Aids: Use flashcards or draw shapes on the board as you say each word. could have vocab art gallery with each student drawing their favorite shape on a white board then going through every artwork and repeating each of them in english between you and the students.

Repetition: Have the children repeat each shape’s name multiple times.

Practice Through Activities (15 minutes)

Shape Hunt: Ask the children to find objects in the room that match the shape you name.

--Objective: Reinforce shape vocabulary by associating it with real-life objects.

Shape Drawing: Provide paper and ask the children to draw each shape as you name it.

--Objective: Practice shape recognition and following instructions in English.

Introduction to Simple Sentences (10 minutes)

Focus: Form simple sentences like "This is a circle," "The window is a square."

Pair Work: Have children practice these sentences with a partner, pointing to different shapes in the classroom or their drawings.

Quiz: Draw or hold up a shape and ask the children to name it in English. also ask color of the shape to review colors vocab

Reinforcement: Review all the shapes learned by asking students to recall them without visual aids.

End with 'Goodbye' in English
